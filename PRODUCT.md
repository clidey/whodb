# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Database administrators and DevOps engineers managing multiple database systems. They work across PostgreSQL, MySQL, MongoDB, Redis, Elasticsearch, ClickHouse, and more — often during incidents or scheduled maintenance. Context: time-pressured, switching between databases frequently, need instant schema comprehension and fast query execution. Ranges from single-DB debugging to multi-system oversight.

## Product Purpose

A lightweight, fast database management tool that makes multi-database administration effortless. WhoDB replaces the friction of legacy tools with instant startup, clean schema visualization, and conversational AI queries — all in under 50MB. Success: a DBA reaches for WhoDB first because it gets out of the way faster than anything else.

## Brand Personality

Confident, clean, capable. Professional tool confidence without unnecessary flair — clarity and speed ARE the personality. Speaks engineer-to-engineer with quiet authority. Never showy, never apologetic. The interface communicates competence through restraint.

## Anti-references

- **phpMyAdmin / legacy tools**: Cluttered, dated, unpolished. The "I have no choice" aesthetic.
- **Enterprise bloat (Oracle/IBM)**: Overwhelming menus, feature overload, corporate gray, wizard-driven workflows that assume incompetence.
- **Overly playful SaaS**: Illustration-heavy landing pages, rounded pastels, startup energy that undermines credibility with infrastructure professionals.

## Design Principles

1. **Speed is the feature** — Every interaction should feel instant. Perceived performance matters as much as actual performance. No loading states that could be avoided.
2. **Density without clutter** — Show information efficiently. DBAs need to see data, not chrome. But never sacrifice scannability for compression.
3. **Quiet confidence** — The tool doesn't explain itself. Controls are where you expect them. Patterns are consistent. Expertise is assumed.
4. **One tool, many databases** — The interface adapts to the database type without fragmenting into per-database UIs. Consistency across engines.
5. **Get out of the way** — Minimize modal interruptions, wizard flows, and confirmation friction. Trust the user. Surface power without gatekeeping it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Standard contrast ratios (4.5:1 body text, 3:1 large text/UI components), full keyboard navigation, screen reader support for all interactive elements. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` and `prefers-color-scheme`. Dark and light modes are first-class — no afterthought theming.
